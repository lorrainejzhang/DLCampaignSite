Dave Singh

I'm a junior in Mechanical & Biomedical Engineering. I'm running to be your Student Body President because I want to help lead Carnegie Mellon to a brighter future and I hope you can help me get there. Most of what I do and what I've done is to further my goal of helping people wherever I am. I'm in MechE/BME because I want to help people who have medical impairments regain their ability to move. I'm an RA in Hamerschlag because I want to help first-years find their passions, pursue their dreams, be caught up with all the resources CMU has to offer, and feel comfortable in their living space. I am a CMU student because I want to make my mark on the world and there's no place I'd rather learn how.

As SBP, as an RA, as a CMU student, and as a human being, I will represent you and your interests, no matter what community or background you come from. You are all valid. You all deserve to have your thoughts and beliefs heard.


Lorraine Zhang

I'm a sophomore Information Systems major, and I'm currently serving as the Communications Chair of Student Senate. I'm running to be your Student Body Vice Preident because I'm passionate about making this campus a community where everyone can feel respected, included, and valued and where everyone has access to the resources they need to thrive and succeed.

This begins with a Student government that understands and reacts to your needs. As Student Body Vice President, I will work to make your voices heard and keep Student Government transparent and accountable to you, and use my experience in Student Government to make a tangible impact on your student experience.


Engage

Making student government a body that understands and responds to your needs by incorporating your voices into the work we do.

Uplift

Creating a community where everyone can feel welcome and actively encouraging collaborative campus growth and dialogue.

Empower

Empowering all students to become leaders in their organizations, communities, and personal lives and strengthening campus resources.

Engage

  • Increasing transparency through regular updates on the issues and initiatives Student Government is working on
  • Including student input on important decisions
  • Actively seeking out and collecting information on what matters to the student body through office hours, posters, polls, and anonymous channels of communication
  • Holding Student Government accountable to the needs of students
  • Working with all of Student Government to set goals and action plans that reflect the current needs and concerns of the students

Uplift

  • Working towards improved resources for students of all backgrounds
  • Improving the international student experience through reducing CPT costs and increasing employment opportunities and resources
  • Expanding resources for low-income students, including foodsharing and food pantry access, housing support, and streamlining the financial aid process
  • Expanding and improving resources like UHS, CAPS, and the Title IX office
  • Hosting events and initiatives to increase collaboration between colleges and disciplines

Empower

  • Publicizing and hosting new opportunities and resources for CMU students to develop skills and become leaders
  • Working with organization leaders to promote collaboration and define and work towards common, long-term goals
  • Working with organizations and campus offices to promote an environment that is welcoming and safe for all
  • Centralizing resources through a Student Government website and app
  • Providing students with the institutional knowledge to create change and improve campus
